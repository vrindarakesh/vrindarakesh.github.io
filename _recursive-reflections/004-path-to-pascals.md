Sometimes a simple puzzle hides deep mathematics.

Imagine standing at the corner of a grid city.
You want to reach the opposite corner.

You can only move:
→ right  
↓ down

How many different paths are possible?

At first it seems like a maze puzzle.
But something beautiful appears.

The numbers that count the paths form **Pascal's Triangle**.

And Pascal's triangle is deeply connected to **binomial coefficients**.
